WALKTHROUGH VIDEO SCRIPT
Patient Acquisition & Growth Agent — Take-Home Assignment
========================================================
Target length: under 3 minutes


SECTION 1 — Delivery Plan Structure (~15 sec)
----------------------------------------------
I structured the engagement across six workstreams — discovery, integration,
conversation design, compliance, QA, and rollout — running as parallel tracks with
explicit dependencies, not a linear sequence. Each has a single owner and specific
outputs that gate downstream work.


SECTION 2 — Milestones and Dependencies (~15 sec)
--------------------------------------------------
The 8-week plan is built around the critical path. The key decision was opening
compliance at Week 3, in parallel with the build. In healthcare AI, compliance is
the most common late blocker — so it gets its own milestone with a hard close date
before QA, not after.


SECTION 3 — Top Risks (~15 sec)
---------------------------------
The two highest-impact risks are EHR integration complexity — if the vendor doesn't
expose write APIs, the agent can't book at all — and compliance exposure. Both are
mitigated structurally and front-loaded into the first three weeks, not discovered
at go-live.


SECTION 4 — Technical Readiness (~15 sec)
------------------------------------------
I focused on scheduling integration because it blocks everything else. The note
defines exactly what "ready to build" means — required inputs, ten questions that
must be answered, and a concrete Week 1 action the integration engineer can send
to client IT immediately.


SECTION 5 — The Prototype (~75 sec)
-------------------------------------
The prototype is a locally runnable launch-readiness tracker built with Python and
Streamlit — something a delivery team would open before a weekly steering call.

At the top, a summary bar shows three live metrics: overall readiness percentage,
blocked milestones, and days to go-live — calculated automatically from the data.

Below that, an editable milestone table covers all eight deliverymilestones. The
Status column has a dropdown for inline updates, and the Blocker column is free-text
so escalation context lives right next to the milestone it affects.

Finally, a go/no-go checklist shows 17 launch criteria across five categories —
Integration, Compliance, Conversation Design, QA, and Operations. Each has a status
dropdown, category completion percentages update live, and the overall readiness score
at the top recalculates automatically.

Clicking Save writes everything to the data file and refreshes the page instantly.


SECTION 6 — AI Tools (~10 sec)
--------------------------------
I used Claude to pressure-test structure, expand written deliverables, and iterate on
the Streamlit code. I made the judgment calls; Claude handled execution speed.


SECTION 7 — Next Steps (~10 sec)
----------------------------------
With more time: surface the risk register inside the app, and run a scheduling rules
workshop with the client's front-desk team to produce actual conversation flow
diagrams — the artifact the team needs most before any build begins.
